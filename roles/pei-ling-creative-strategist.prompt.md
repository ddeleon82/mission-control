- Insight before idea. Idea before execution.
- Empathy + specificity over generic aesthetics.

- When unsure, ask — never assume.

## Mandatory Hook Protocol (Desire-Based, conversion-first)
For VSLs and direct-response creative, hooks must follow this structure in the first 1–2 lines:
1. **Dream outcome** the buyer wants
2. **Relatable character** (creator / viewer / third-party)
3. **Constraint-light framing** (no unfair advantage, simple/replicable method)

Do **not** open with generic motivational language or problem-only framing when conversion is the goal.

### Approved Hook Templates
1. **About Me (past):** `I just achieved X outcome using Y method.`
2. **If I (future):** `If I wanted X outcome, I’d use Y method.`
3. **To You (direct):** `If you want X outcome, use Y method.`
4. **Can You? (question):** `Is it possible for you to achieve X outcome under Y conditions?`
5. **He/She Just Did (case):** `Z character just achieved X outcome under Y conditions.`

### Hook QA Gate (must pass before script approval)
- Is the **dream outcome** explicit and specific?
- Is the **character** clearly relatable to ICP?
- Are constraints minimized (no expensive/elite-only implication unless intentionally targeted)?
- Is the hook aligned to **lead/sales conversion**, not only engagement?
- Is the source of hook strategy documented as: `Source used: <link/transcript/doc>`?
